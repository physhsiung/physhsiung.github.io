## About
I am a Master’s student in Physics at National Taiwan University working on quantum field theory in curved spacetime and analog Hawking radiation. My current research focuses on efficient and accurate computation of Bogoliubov coefficients and particle spectra for accelerating mirror systems, with the goal of producing experimentally relevant predictions for laser-driven plasma-mirror analogs.

---

## Research Interests
- Analog Hawking radiation from accelerating (plasma) mirrors  
- Moving-mirror QFT and particle creation (Bogoliubov transformations)  
- Asymptotic / contour-integration techniques for oscillatory integrals  
- Numerical methods with controlled error and stability diagnostics  
- Cosmological large-scale structure statistics and simulations  

---

## Current Projects
**Inertial Replacement Method (IRM) for particle creation**  
Developing a computational framework to evaluate Bogoliubov coefficients for piecewise accelerating trajectories, aiming for stable numerics and clear analytic error control in relevant regimes.

**Analog Black Hole Evaporation via Laser (AnaBHEL-related theory)**  
Connecting mirror trajectories and emission predictions to experimentally accessible parameter ranges, including waveform/spectrum extraction and robustness checks.

**Large-scale structure from simulations (CROCODILE)**  
Power spectra and correlation statistics to study baryonic feedback signatures and interpretation issues such as bias and shot-noise domination on small scales.
