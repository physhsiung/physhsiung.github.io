Research

My work connects quantum field theory in curved spacetime, analogue gravity, and cosmology, with an emphasis on particle creation, symmetry constraints, and numerical methods.

Research Areas Publications Presentations

Research Areas

Particle Creation and Moving Mirrors

I study particle creation in accelerating-mirror systems, which serve as analogue models for black-hole radiation. I am especially interested in stable numerical and semi-analytic methods for computing Bogoliubov coefficients for experimentally realistic trajectories.

The Inertial Replacement Method (IRM) is a framework for treating long-time oscillatory integrals by replacing asymptotic regions with analytically controlled inertial segments, while computing the physically relevant accelerating region numerically.

Analogue Hawking radiation Moving mirrors Bogoliubov coefficients Numerical methods Oscillatory integrals Inertial Replacement Method

Cosmological Correlators and Bootstrap

I am interested in cosmological correlators and the cosmological bootstrap: using symmetry principles to constrain correlation functions that encode inflationary physics in the early universe.

Cosmological correlators Cosmological bootstrap Inflation Symmetry constraints

Cosmology and Large-Scale Structure

I also work on cosmological simulations and statistics of large-scale structure, including matter power spectra and baryonic feedback effects.

Large-scale structure Power spectra Baryonic feedback
