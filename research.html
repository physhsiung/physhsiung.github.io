Research

Particle Creation and Moving Mirrors

My current research focuses on particle creation in accelerating-mirror systems, which serve as analogue models for black-hole radiation. I am especially interested in developing stable numerical and semi-analytic methods for computing Bogoliubov coefficients for experimentally realistic trajectories.

Inertial Replacement Method

The Inertial Replacement Method (IRM) is a framework for treating long-time oscillatory integrals by replacing asymptotic regions with analytically controlled inertial segments, while computing the physically relevant accelerating region numerically.

Cosmology and Large-Scale Structure

I also work on cosmological simulations and statistics of large-scale structure, including matter power spectra and baryonic feedback effects.
